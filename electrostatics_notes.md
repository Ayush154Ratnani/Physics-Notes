## Q1. Define linear charge density, surface charge density and volume charge density. Write their SI units.

When electric charge is distributed over a body, its distribution is described using charge densities.

1. **Linear Charge Density (lambda):**
   It is defined as the amount of electric charge per unit length of a charged conductor or wire.
   Formula: lambda = dq/dl
   SI unit: coulomb per metre (C m^-1)

2. **Surface Charge Density (sigma):**
   It is the amount of electric charge distributed per unit area of a charged surface.
   Formula: sigma = dq/dA
   SI unit: coulomb per square metre (C m^-2)

3. **Volume Charge Density (rho):**
   It is the amount of electric charge per unit volume of a charged body.
   Formula: rho = dq/dV
   SI unit: coulomb per cubic metre (C m^-3)

## Q2. State and prove Gauss's law of electrostatics.

### Statement of Gauss's Law:
The total electric flux through any closed surface is equal to (1/epsilon0) times the total charge enclosed by the surface.

Integral form: Integral(E . dS) over closed surface = Q/epsilon0

### Proof:
Consider a point charge q placed at the centre of a spherical Gaussian surface of radius r.

Electric field on the surface:
E = (1/4*pi*epsilon0) * (q/r^2)

Electric flux through the surface:
Phi = Integral(E . dS)

Since E is constant over the spherical surface and acts radially outward:
Phi = E * Integral(dS) = E * (4*pi*r^2)

Substitute E:
Phi = (1/4*pi*epsilon0) * (q/r^2) * 4*pi*r^2 = q/epsilon0

Hence, Integral(E . dS) over closed surface = q/epsilon0.
Therefore, Gauss's law is proved.

## Q3. What are electric lines of force? Explain their properties.

Electric lines of force are imaginary continuous curves drawn in an electric field such that the tangent at any point gives the direction of the electric field at that point.

Properties:
1. Electric field lines originate from positive charges and terminate on negative charges.
2. No two electric field lines can intersect each other.
3. The density of field lines represents the strength of the electric field.
4. Electric field lines are perpendicular to the surface of a conductor.
5. Electric field lines do not form closed loops.

## Q4. Define electric field and electric field intensity. Write its SI unit.

Electric field at a point is the region around a charged body in which it exerts a force on another charge.

Electric field intensity is defined as the force experienced by a unit positive test charge placed at that point.
Formula: E = F/q
SI unit: newton per coulomb (N C^-1) or volt per metre (V m^-1)

## Q5. State and explain Coulomb's law and law of conservation of charge.

### Coulomb's Law:
The force of attraction or repulsion between two point charges is:
- directly proportional to the product of their charges
- inversely proportional to the square of the distance between them

Formula: F = (1/4*pi*epsilon0) * (q1*q2/r^2)
The force acts along the line joining the two charges.

### Law of Conservation of Charge:
Electric charge can neither be created nor destroyed, but it can be transferred from one body to another.
The total charge of an isolated system remains constant.

## Q6. What is an electric dipole? Derive the expression for torque acting on an electric dipole placed in a uniform electric field.

An electric dipole consists of two equal and opposite charges separated by a small distance.
Dipole moment: p = q*(2a)

When placed in a uniform electric field E, equal and opposite forces act on the two charges and form a couple.
Magnitude of torque: tau = p*E*sin(theta)
where theta is the angle between dipole moment and electric field.

The torque tends to align the dipole in the direction of the electric field.

## Q7. Derive expression for electric field intensity on the axial line of an electric dipole.

Consider a dipole with charges +q and -q separated by distance 2a.
Let point P be at distance r from the centre on the axial line.

Net electric field at P:
E = (1/4*pi*epsilon0) * [q/(r-a)^2 - q/(r+a)^2]

For r >> a, after simplification:
E_axial = (1/4*pi*epsilon0) * (2p/r^3)

Direction of the field is along the dipole moment.

## Q8. State Coulomb's law in vector form and explain the terms.

Vector form of Coulomb's law:
F12 = (1/4*pi*epsilon0) * (q1*q2/r^2) * rhat12

Where:
- rhat12 is the unit vector from charge q1 to q2.
- The force acts along the line joining the two charges.
